Learn About RSV Before Your Baby Does

Currently, there is no RSV vaccine or immunization approved for use in all babies.

RSV can range from mild to severe and can last up to 2 weeks

Respiratory syntactical (sin-SISH-uhl) virus, or RSV, is a common respiratory virus that usually causes mild, cold-like symptoms. Most people recover in a week or two, but RSV can be serious, especially for infants and older adults. RSV is the most common cause of bronchiolitis (inflammation of the small airways in the lung) and pneumonia (infection of the lungs) in children younger than 1 year of age in the United States.
